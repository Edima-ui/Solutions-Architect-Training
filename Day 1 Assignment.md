3 examples of where a technical solution could fail if the business problem is misunderstood: 
(a) Adding the wrong features
Sometimes developers think users want more features, but the real problem is that the system is hard to use.
For example, an online shopping website may add many new options, while customers are actually struggling to complete payment.
it results in the system becomes more complicated, and customers still leave without buying anything.
(b) Automating the wrong process
A company may try to speed up a process without fixing the real issue behind it.
For example, a business automates how customer complaints are handled, but the real problem is that complaints are not properly solved.
it results in complaints being processed faster, but customers are still unhappy.
(c) Solving the wrong problem
Sometimes the focus is on the wrong goal.
For example, a company builds a powerful data dashboard because they think they need more data, but the real problem is poor decision-making.
it results in the system is not used much and does not improve the business.
